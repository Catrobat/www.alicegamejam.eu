Alice Game Jam

ABOUT

150 years of Alice in Wonderland

Lewis Carroll’s Alice in Wonderland was published in 1865. Since then, the adventures of Alice, the White Rabbit and the other fabulous figures in the book have fascinated young and old for 150 years. Now, between the 10th and 18th of October 2015, the Alice Game Jam Europe invites you to recreate the spirit of this magical world by creating fun new games about Alice and her friends. You don’t need any previous experience producing apps or coding; you don’t even need a PC, laptop, or tablet. Your smartphone is enough, and you’ll learn the rest in our tutorials and by exploring what others have created. You’ll then be able to share your own real game apps with your friends and the whole world!

Alice follows a white rabbit into its hole and comes to a magical, imaginative, and exciting world. She experiences some gorgeous and thrilling adventures on her journey through this wonderland, meeting new and marvelous creatures like the Mad Hatter or the Cheshire Cat...

The Game Jam

With Pocket Code we aim to inspire teenagers to create their own games and interactive stories directly on their smartphones. What theme for our first Game Jam could be better than Alice in Wonderland?

Alice’s story is full of fantastic creatures, magical encounters, and an imaginative playground for your own creativity! We invite you to be part of our first Pocket Game Jam and create the most amazing games you can think of!

Pocket Code is free on Google Play!

EVENT

Details

This first Alice Game Jam by Catrobat and Pocket Code took place during the European CodeWeek between October 11th and 18th. Dozens of users have been part of this Game Jam to design and develop almost 200 games and stories about Alice, the Mad Hatter and all the other fabulous characters out of the wonderland!

After the great success of this Game Jam we even decided to organize more Game Jams of this kind! Subscribe to our newsletter, follow "Pocket Code" on Facebook, Twitter or Google + or visit this website again in a couple of weeks to stay tuned about further Game James with Pocket Code!

Europe Code Week

The Europe Code Week is an European Initiative to promote coding and to make it easy for everyone to learn how to code. Events all over Europe take place during this particular week to spread this future oriented idea within schools, public events or workshops. Further information about the initiative, the people behind it and all possibilities can be found on codeweek.eu!

The Catrobat Project

If you want to learn more about the nonprofit Catrobat project - the developers of Pocket Code - take a look at the website www.catrobat.org to find all information about the project, the team behind it and of course news about future events!

PARTICIPATE

Where can I find the submitted projects?

There have been so many great projects that we want to share them with you! Find all Alice-themed Games, Stories or Animations on the Pocket Code Website! You may open and play them directly on your Android-phone wit Pocket Code by simply clicking on it!

If you want to share your finished project with your friends, family or community just copy the link on the website and share it on Facebook, Twitter, Google+ or any other media. You can also download these games to remix or extend them - let your ideas become real, even when this event is already over!

How to learn coding?

If you don't have any experience in coding don't be afraid, it's easier as you might think! At the Tutorial Section of Pocket Code there are some great examples and tipps for beginners. Furthermore on the website of Pocket Code are many games and examples provided which you may use to learn, expand or remix them.

Find the right materials for your game

To make it easier for you we will provide some special media assets which you may use during the Game Jam. To get more information about free media-sources take a look at our FAQs.

OUR PARTNERS

GameCity

Based in Nottingham (UK), GameCity has worked for 10 years to explore and celebrate the culture, history and creativity of videogames. Its projects include the annual GameCity Festival, the GameCity Prize, and the new National Videogame Arcade.

If you want to know more about GameCity visit www.gamecity.org.

Catrobat

Catrobat is a non-profit project providing free educational apps for children and teenagers.

If you want to know more about Catrobat visit www.catrobat.org.

No One Left Behind project

The “No One Left Behind” project tackles key challenges in the education sector by using the potential of digital games and game-making. The project will unlock inclusive gaming creation and experiences in formal learning situations, underpin meaningful learning, and support children to realise their full potential.

This project has received funding from the European Union’s Seventh Framework Programme for research, technological development and demonstration under grant agreement no 645215.

For further information visit www.no1leftbehind.eu

British Library

The British Library is the largest library of the world by catalogued items and one of the most important in the fields of research. It is also the home of the original Alice in Wonderland books and provides a wide range of resources about Alice and her adventures. The Discovering Literature section provides lots of opportunities to find exciting and new facts about Alice, e.g., Understanding Alice, Anthropomorphism in Alice's Adventures in Wonderland, or Eating and Drinking in Alice's Adventures in Wonderland.
